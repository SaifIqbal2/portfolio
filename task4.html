Tool Choice and Rationale

Tool Choice and Rationale

The assessment criteria required on the structure of an e portfolio, its accessibility and interactivity forced me to use HTML, CSS and JavaScript to design this e portfolio. This strategy gives complete management over the semantic headings, reactive design and additions of a lightbox, tooltips, collapsible pages, tabs and scroll bars. It is also compatible with a simple deployment to GitHub pages or Netlify without vendor lock in.

The best practice principles were applied in the design decision-making choices so that a clear purpose, simple navigation, and evidence rich artifacts could be seen as the means to make learning visible through reflection and organising [1]. The research on high impact e portfolio also emphasizes that the most useful portfolios are the ones that combine academic work with professional identity and portfolios that grow as the time passes, as opposed to those that are uploaded separately [2]. According to this recommendation, every task page in this section comprises a dedicated narrative, supporting images with captions, and an IEEE reference list.
